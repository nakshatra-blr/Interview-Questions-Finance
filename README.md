## 📖 Accounting Basics

### Q1. What are the 3 Golden Rules of Accounting?
**Answer:**
| Account Type | Rule |
|---|---|
| Personal | Debit the Receiver, Credit the Giver |
| Real | Debit what Comes In, Credit what Goes Out |
| Nominal | Debit Expenses & Losses, Credit Incomes & Gains |

### Q2. What is the difference between Cash and Accrual accounting?
**Answer:**
| | Cash Basis | Accrual Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue recorded | When cash received | When earned |
| Expense recorded | When cash paid | When incurred |
| Used by | Small businesses | Large companies (mandatory) |
| Accuracy | Less accurate | More accurate |

### Q3. What are the 3 financial statements?
**Answer:**
1. **Income Statement (P&L)** — Shows profit/loss over a period
2. **Balance Sheet** — Shows assets, liabilities & equity at a point in time
3. **Cash Flow Statement** — Shows actual cash movement

### Q4. What is the accounting equation?
**Answer:** `Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity`

### Q5. What is depreciation? Name the methods.
**Answer:** Systematic reduction of asset value over useful life.
- Straight Line Method (SLM): Equal amount each year
- Written Down Value (WDV): Percentage of remaining value
- Units of Production: Based on actual usage

### Q6. What is the difference between Debtors and Creditors?
**Answer:**
- **Debtors** — People who OWE us money (Asset)
- **Creditors** — People to whom WE owe money (Liability)

### Q7. What is Working Capital?
**Answer:** `Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities`
It measures short-term liquidity and operational efficiency.

### Q8. Explain FIFO, LIFO, and Weighted Average.
**Answer:** Inventory valuation methods:
- **FIFO** — First items purchased are sold first (most common in India)
- **LIFO** — Last items purchased are sold first (not allowed under Ind AS)
- **Weighted Average** — Average cost of all items

### Q9. What is a Trial Balance?
**Answer:** A statement listing all ledger account balances to verify that total debits = total credits. It's a check (not proof) of arithmetical accuracy.

### Q10. What is Bank Reconciliation Statement (BRS)?
**Answer:** A statement prepared to reconcile the difference between the bank balance as per Cash Book and as per Bank Passbook. Differences arise due to cheques issued but not presented, cheques deposited but not cleared, bank charges, etc.

## 📊 Financial Analysis

### Q11. What are the key financial ratios?
**Answer:**
| Category | Key Ratios |
|---|---|
| Liquidity | Current Ratio, Quick Ratio |
| Profitability | Net Margin, ROE, ROCE |
| Solvency | Debt/Equity, Interest Coverage |
| Efficiency | Inventory Turnover, Receivable Days |

### Q12. How do you analyze if a company is financially healthy?
**Answer:** Check these 5 things:
1. Consistent revenue growth (>10% YoY)
2. Positive and growing net profit
3. Debt/Equity < 1 (low leverage)
4. Operating cash flow > Net income
5. ROE > 15% (generating good returns)

### Q13. What is EBITDA and why is it important?
**Answer:** Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation & Amortization. It shows core operational profitability without the effect of financing decisions, tax structure, or accounting policies.

### Q14. Difference between Gross Profit and Net Profit?
**Answer:**
- **Gross Profit** = Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold (direct costs only)
- **Net Profit** = Revenue - ALL expenses (COGS + operating + interest + tax)

### Q15. What is Cash Flow vs Profit? Can a profitable company run out of cash?
**Answer:** YES! Profit is an accounting concept (accrual basis), but cash flow is actual money movement. A company can show profit on paper but have no cash if customers haven't paid yet (high receivables) or if it overspent on inventory/assets.

## 🧾 Taxation (India)

### Q16. What are the 5 heads of income under Income Tax?
**Answer:**
1. Income from Salary
2. Income from House Property
3. Profits & Gains from Business/Profession
4. Capital Gains (Short-term & Long-term)
5. Income from Other Sources

### Q17. Difference between TDS and TCS?
**Answer:**
- **TDS** — Tax Deducted at Source (deducted by PAYER)
- **TCS** — Tax Collected at Source (collected by SELLER)

### Q18. What is the difference between CGST, SGST, and IGST?
**Answer:**
- **CGST + SGST** — Applied on intra-state transactions (within same state)
- **IGST** — Applied on inter-state transactions (between different states)
- Total GST rate remains the same in both cases

### Q19. What is Input Tax Credit (ITC)?
**Answer:** The credit a business can claim on GST paid on purchases/inputs, which reduces the GST liability on output/sales. Conditions: Must have valid invoice, goods/services received, returns filed.

### Q20. What is the difference between Tax Evasion and Tax Avoidance?
**Answer:**
- **Tax Avoidance** — Legal; using loopholes to reduce tax (e.g., investing in 80C)
- **Tax Evasion** — Illegal; hiding income, fake expenses (punishable by law)

## 🏦 Banking & Finance

### Q21. What does RBI do?
**Answer:** Reserve Bank of India functions:
- Monetary policy (controls inflation via repo rate)
- Issues currency
- Banker to government and banks
- Regulates banking system
- Manages foreign exchange reserves

### Q22. What is Repo Rate and Reverse Repo Rate?
**Answer:**
- **Repo Rate** — Rate at which RBI lends to commercial banks (currently ~6.5%)
- **Reverse Repo Rate** — Rate at which banks deposit surplus with RBI

When RBI increases Repo Rate → Loans become expensive → Inflation controlled

### Q23. What is NPA (Non-Performing Asset)?
**Answer:** A loan where the borrower has stopped paying interest or principal for more than 90 days. Categories:
- Sub-standard (up to 12 months)
- Doubtful (12-36 months)
- Loss (above 36 months)

### Q24. Difference between FDI and FII?
**Answer:**
- **FDI** — Foreign Direct Investment (long-term, controlling stake, builds business)
- **FII** — Foreign Institutional Investment (short-term, stock market, portfolio investment)

### Q25. What is Mutual Fund? Types?
**Answer:** Pool of money from multiple investors managed by professionals.
- **Equity Funds** — Invest in stocks (high risk, high return)
- **Debt Funds** — Invest in bonds (low risk, stable returns)
- **Hybrid Funds** — Mix of equity and debt
- **Index Funds** — Mirror market index (passive, low cost)

## 💻 Excel & Tools

### Q26. What is VLOOKUP? When does it fail?
**Answer:** Looks up a value in the first column of a range and returns a value in the same row from another column.
`=VLOOKUP(lookup_value, table_array, col_index, [range_lookup])`
**Fails when:** Lookup value is to the RIGHT of the return value (use INDEX-MATCH instead).

### Q27. What is a Pivot Table?
**Answer:** A data summarization tool that can automatically sort, count, total, or average data in a spreadsheet. Used to transform raw data into meaningful reports without formulas.

### Q28. What Excel functions do you use most in accounting?
**Answer:** SUMIF, VLOOKUP, INDEX-MATCH, IF, CONCATENATE, TEXT, EOMONTH, pivot tables, conditional formatting, data validation.

### Q29. Have you used Tally? What for?
**Answer:** Yes — for recording journal entries, maintaining ledgers, generating Trial Balance, preparing financial statements, GST return filing (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B), TDS calculations, and inventory management.

### Q30. What is the difference between Excel and Tally?
**Answer:**
- **Excel** — General purpose, flexible, manual (for analysis, modeling)
- **Tally** — Purpose-built for accounting (auto-posting, GST compliance, reports)

## 🎭 Situational Questions

### Q36. You find an error in last month's financial report that was already submitted. What do you do?
**Answer:** Immediately inform my supervisor, assess the materiality of the error, prepare a corrected version with notes explaining the change, and follow the company's restatement policy. Transparency > hiding mistakes.

### Q37. A client asks you to adjust numbers to reduce their tax liability illegally. What do you do?
**Answer:** Politely but firmly refuse. Explain that it's illegal (tax evasion) and offer legitimate tax planning alternatives within the law. If they insist, escalate to my senior/manager.

### Q38. How would you handle a tight deadline for closing monthly accounts?
**Answer:** Prioritize critical entries first, use templates for recurring entries, communicate early if I foresee delays, ask for help if needed, and stay focused. Planning and time management are key — most deadline stress comes from poor planning.
